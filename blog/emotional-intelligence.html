My Reflections: Mindfulness

Do you have any biases for or against mindfulness? If so, can you describe them?

I defintely have a bias for. I have been practicing mindfulness in various forms for a long time. Having said that though, it's often a practice whereby I have to consciously make myself do it. It's a bit like exercise, I have to remind myself of how I feel afterwards. Tasks tend to be my main focus and I want to just jump straight in and begin. So I also have to tell myself that to practice my mindfulness will actually aid me in my tasks.

How did you find the overall experience?

Very pleasant and helpful. I have one breathing exercise meditation that I practice and I figured that was enough. The exercise showed me that i can have multiple and the benefits are worth it.

What's your daily habit commitment for mindfulness?

I am committing to practicing mindfulness every morning before I begin my day's work at my work station.

Listening and Looping

What was it like to be fully listened to?

It's always a little disconcerting, like you're doing a performance. You want to do it perfectly and if you lose focus or go off on a tangent, it can shake your sense of what you're trying to get across.

What was it like to fully listen?

A good experience and something that I actively work on anyway. I'm wary though of taking mental notes as I go. I found that if I just listened without any internal monlogue, I was actually able to recall those parts more accurately.
